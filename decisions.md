## 2026-04-27 ‚Äî Real estate becomes a second business line; receipts reuse `client_payment` with a deal-link FK
**What:** New `/real-estate` route holds rent and sale agreements. Schema additions are minimal: `real_estate_deals` (single-currency, free-text label, `sub_type in ('rent','sale')`, `contact_id`), `real_estate_installments` (deal_id, due_date, expected_amount, sequence) for the schedule, and two columns on `transactions`: `real_estate_deal_id uuid` (CHECK forces `kind='client_payment'` when set) and `revenue_source text` (`real_estate` | `export` | NULL). Receipts are NOT a new transaction kind ‚Äî they're regular `client_payment` rows with `real_estate_deal_id` and `revenue_source='real_estate'` set by the `createReceipt` mutation. Installment status is computed at read time by `allocateRealEstateInstallments` (FIFO, oldest-first; mirrors `allocatePartnerReimbursements`). A new contact type `real_estate` flows through `CONTACT_TYPES` so buyers/tenants are filterable. The dashboard gets a fourth attention rule: any deal installment more than 7 days overdue. Profit & Loss's revenue card splits into `Export` and `Real estate` sublines using a three-rule predicate: `real_estate_deal_id != null` ‚Üí real_estate; else `revenue_source` if set; else `contact.type='real_estate'` ‚Üí real_estate; else export.
**Why:** Real estate cashflows look identical to client payments in every operational way ‚Äî money arrives in an existing account, the counterparty is a contact, and treasury/PSD pipelines must keep working unchanged. Forking a new transaction kind would have meant duplicating mutation/spawn-movement/contact-ledger code for no semantic benefit. The deal-link FK plus a tag column gives the P&L split without polluting the kind enum. Single-currency-per-deal is a simplification justified by Yusuf's volume (~tens of deals, not hundreds); two parallel deals is an acceptable workaround if a multi-currency contract ever appears. The 7-day overdue threshold (vs 30 days for partner reimbursements) reflects rent's faster slip pattern.
**Alternatives:** (a) New transaction kind `real_estate_payment` ‚Äî rejected, would fork the contact-ledger query, the spawn-movement path, and the FX block on client_payment for nothing. (b) Properties registry with per-property P&L ‚Äî rejected per Yusuf, the free-text deal label carries the identity well enough at this volume. (c) Multi-currency deals via a JSON installment column ‚Äî rejected, single-currency keeps the FIFO allocator straightforward and matches every signed contract he showed. (d) Document attachments on deals (s√∂zle≈üme, taahh√ºtname) ‚Äî deferred; Storage bucket pattern from shipments is reusable when needed. (e) Auto-rolling rent renewals ‚Äî rejected, renewal becomes a new deal so historical reporting stays clean.

## 2026-04-27 ‚Äî Shipment net profit recognized at booking via three transactions (billing, COGS, freight)
**What:** The `draft ‚Üí booked` transition now writes three transactions, all dated today and linked via `related_shipment_id`: `shipment_billing` (sales only ‚Äî freight removed from the existing computation), a new `shipment_cogs` (Œ£ `coalesce(actual_purchase_price, est_purchase_unit_price, 0) √ó quantity`), and a new `shipment_freight` (the shipment's freight_cost). `supplier_invoice` is removed from `EXPENSE_KINDS` in P&L ‚Äî it's a payable settlement, not a P&L expense ‚Äî and `shipment_cogs` + `shipment_freight` take its place. Both new kinds are added to `DISABLED_KINDS` so the generic transaction form never offers them. They follow the same lifecycle as `shipment_billing`: refresh on stage transitions, freeze at `arrived`. `computeShipmentTotal` is split into `computeSalesTotal` and `computeCogsTotal` for clarity and to fix the existing freight-into-revenue bug.
**Why:** Yusuf considers a shipment's profit "made" the moment it reaches `booked` (cash typically already collected). Pre-fix, only revenue was accrued at booking ‚Äî costs leaked in via `supplier_invoice` whenever the supplier's bill was entered, often weeks later in a different month. The triple-write makes the booking month show the full net deterministically, with no reliance on the user logging the supplier invoice on time. Reclassifying `supplier_invoice` as balance-sheet only is the correct accounting treatment regardless of this change ‚Äî it represents a payable, not an expense.
**Alternatives:** (a) Single `shipment_billing` row whose amount is `sales ‚àí cogs ‚àí freight` ‚Äî rejected, loses per-component visibility in the P&L breakdown. (b) Backfill historical `shipment_billing` rows to remove embedded freight ‚Äî rejected, small dataset, clean break going forward is simpler. (c) Editable `booked_date` to backdate accruals ‚Äî rejected by Yusuf, auto-timestamp is the desired behavior. (d) Keep `supplier_invoice` in P&L and add a "payables" filter ‚Äî rejected, would require teaching every P&L view to exclude unsettled invoices and reintroduces the date-mismatch problem.

## 2026-04-27 ‚Äî Loans become first-class on `/partners`; `is_loan` flag on transactions disambiguates from reimbursements
**What:** The Loans placeholder on `/partners` is replaced by a real strip + dialog + schedule drawer mirroring the PSD section's shape. Scope is intentionally narrow ‚Äî company ‚Üí partner only, single-leg per loan, no interest, principal-only, with an optional installment schedule for nudging. New schema: a boolean `is_loan` column on `transactions` (defaults false; CHECK constrains it to `partner_loan_in`/`partner_loan_out` rows with a `partner_id`) plus a `loan_installments` child table keyed to the disbursement transaction. `partner_loan_out + is_loan=true` is a loan disbursement; `partner_loan_in + is_loan=true` is a repayment. Reimbursement payouts (the existing 2026-04-24 flow) keep `is_loan=false` and the FIFO allocator at `pending-reimbursements.ts:63` and `partner-reimbursement-summary.ts:24` now skips `is_loan=true` rows. Repayments do not store an explicit loan link ‚Äî outstanding is computed per (partner, currency) by summing `partner_loan_out` with `is_loan=true` and subtracting `partner_loan_in` with `is_loan=true`; installment status is computed by FIFO-walking repayments oldest-first against installments oldest-first across that bucket's loans. Loans support full edit + delete (matching PSD); CASCADE on the FK drops installments with the disbursement. The detail-page activity-sums grid splits the old combined "Loans returned & reimbursements paid" card into four cards: Capital received (`partner_loan_in` && !is_loan), Loan repayments received (`partner_loan_in` && is_loan), Reimbursements paid (`partner_loan_out` && !is_loan), Partner-paid expenses (unchanged).
**Why:** The 2026-04-24 deferral was driven by exactly one technical blocker ‚Äî `partner_loan_out` was overloaded between loan disbursements and reimbursement payouts, so the FIFO reimbursement engine would silently consume loans as if they settled claims. A boolean flag resolves this without forking the kind enum, splitting the table, or touching the existing reimbursement allocator's algorithm. Single-leg-per-loan is justified by frequency (Yusuf's stated "once a year or less"); a multi-leg event header would add table surface for no observable benefit. Storing installments in a child table (rather than a JSON column on transactions) keeps each installment query-addressable for FIFO matching and per-row status badges. Per-(partner, currency) outstanding rather than per-loan FIFO matching keeps the compute simple at our row volumes and matches the same modeling pragmatism `decisions.md` chose for partner reimbursements.
**Alternatives:** (a) New `partner_loans` header table mirroring `psd_events` ‚Äî rejected, single-leg means the disbursement transaction is already the loan record; a header table would add reconciliation complexity for no flexibility we need. (b) Keep both directions (partner‚Üícompany AND company‚Üípartner) symmetric in the UI ‚Äî rejected, partner‚Üícompany loans are unambiguous (no kind overlap) and already recordable via the Transactions module; the Loans section's job is solving the disambiguation problem. (c) Per-loan FIFO outstanding tracking ‚Äî rejected, partner+currency bucketing is enough for the "how much is owed" question and matches the rest of partner-flow modeling. (d) Manual repayment-to-loan linking ‚Äî rejected, adds friction at every repayment for accuracy nobody asked for. (e) Track interest and maturity dates as structured fields ‚Äî rejected, owner loans are zero-interest in this business; installment due dates carry the same nudge value with less surface. (f) Backfill historical `partner_loan_out` rows by hand ‚Äî explicitly chosen against; the migration defaults `is_loan=false`, which preserves all existing rows as reimbursement payouts (their actual past use). New loans get `is_loan=true` going forward.


## 2026-04-27 ‚Äî PSD reshaped around the event, never the partner; multi-leg per event
**What:** A new `psd_events` parent table holds per-event metadata (`event_date`, optional `fiscal_period` as `YYYY` / `YYYY-QN` / `YYYY-MM`, optional `note`). Each `profit_distribution` transaction now carries `psd_event_id` (FK, ON DELETE CASCADE) and `partner_id` is forbidden on these rows by a CHECK constraint. One PSD event can hold multiple legs across currencies and source accounts (e.g. 100k TRY from Bank-A + 5k USD from Bank-C in a single event). Logging is funneled exclusively through a new `PsdEventDialog` on `/partners` ‚Äî `profit_distribution` is added to `DISABLED_KINDS` so the generic transaction form no longer offers it. The calendar drawer now renders each populated cell as a popover that lists the events behind it with an Edit button per event; deletes happen from inside the dialog and cascade-remove the legs and their treasury movements.
**Why:** Yusuf's mental model for PSD is "how much money has left the company to the owners" ‚Äî not "what did Partner 2 get." The previous shape (one `profit_distribution` row per partner, optional `partner_id`) invited per-partner views that don't match how he reasons about distributions, and the form let users accidentally create partner-tagged PSDs. Storing the event as a first-class parent with N legs preserves the existing `transactions ‚Üí treasury_movements` pipeline (each leg debits the right account in the right currency) while keeping the question answerable as a single SQL filter (`kind='profit_distribution' AND psd_event_id IS NOT NULL`). Disabling the kind in the generic form means there is exactly one creation path, so the no-partner invariant can't be bypassed.
**Alternatives:** (a) One transaction with N "leg" rows in a JSON column ‚Äî rejected, treasury movements need a real `from_account_id` per leg, JSON would need shadow rows anyway. (b) Three transactions per event (one per partner, equal split) auto-generated ‚Äî rejected by Yusuf, this is exactly the per-partner structure he doesn't want. (c) Keep `partner_id` optional on `profit_distribution` and rely on UI discipline ‚Äî rejected, schema-level invariants beat UI discipline; the CHECK constraint guarantees no PSD leg can ever sneak a partner. (d) Dedicated `/psd` route ‚Äî rejected, monthly cadence doesn't justify a top-level entry; the partners-page strip is enough. (e) Append-only edits with reversing entries ‚Äî rejected, Yusuf wants direct edit/delete; the audit trail through `created_time`/`edited_time` is sufficient for a small-team owner-managed business.

## 2026-04-25 ‚Äî `accounts` lifecycle uses `is_active` + `deleted_at`, mirroring partners
**What:** Added two columns to `public.accounts`: `is_active boolean not null default true` (deactivate hides the account from new-transaction and new-movement pickers) and `deleted_at timestamptz null` (soft-delete hides it from the registry list). Both states are reversible from the UI; there is no hard delete from the page. A partial index `(id) where deleted_at is null and is_active = true` powers the picker chokepoint, and a partial unique index on `lower(account_name) where deleted_at is null` enforces case-insensitive uniqueness while letting a soft-deleted name be reused. **Why:** `treasury_movements` and historical `transactions` reference accounts by FK and must survive retirement of the underlying wallet. The two-tier lifecycle (deactivate vs soft-delete) is the same rationale as the 2026-04-23 partners decision: deactivate retires from pickers without removing from the registry view, while soft-delete archives from the registry without breaking historical references. Mirroring the partners pattern keeps mental load low across modules.
**Alternatives:** Single `is_active` flag only ‚Äî rejected, no way to retire-and-archive separately (an archived wallet still clutters the registry list). Hard delete with cascade ‚Äî rejected, would orphan or destroy `treasury_movements` history. Boolean `is_archived` plus `is_active` ‚Äî rejected, indistinguishable from `is_active` + `deleted_at` but loses the "when did this happen" timestamp that `deleted_at` provides for free.

## 2026-04-26 ‚Äî KDV summary is computed on-the-fly from transactions; no materialized period table
**What:** The `/tax` page computes monthly KDV totals by pulling the last 12 months of VAT-bearing transactions (`supplier_invoice`, `expense`, `other_expense`, `shipment_billing`, `other_income`) plus any `tax_payment` rows and running them through `summarizeKdv` ‚Äî a pure function in `src/lib/ledger/kdv-summary.ts`. ## 2026-04-26 ‚Äî `transactions.kdv_period` is the only KDV metadata; restricted to `tax_payment` rows
**What:** A single `text` column `kdv_period` (shape `YYYY-MM`) on `public.transactions`, guarded by two CHECK constraints: (1) format regex `^[0-9]{4}-(0[1-9]|1[0-2])$`, (2) `kdv_period IS NULL OR kind = 'tax_payment'`. A partial index covers non-null values. The wizard writes it only when `kind = 'tax_payment'`; all other branches write `null`. On `tax_payment` save it's optional ‚Äî a filing with no `kdv_period` is still a valid row, just unlinked to any month.
**Why:** One column, two constraints, one index ‚Äî the minimum surface needed to answer "did this month get filed, and which payment settled it?". The partial index keeps lookups by period fast without bloating the main index tree. Kind-restriction at the DB layer means a drift bug in the UI can't quietly stamp `kdv_period` onto an unrelated row. Keeping the column on `transactions` (rather than a sibling `tax_payments` table) means the existing transactions list, ledger, and edit flow work unchanged.
**Alternatives:** Sibling `tax_payments` table keyed by `transaction_id` ‚Äî rejected, duplicates row identity for a 1:1 relationship and needs joins everywhere. JSONB `kdv_metadata` blob ‚Äî rejected, loses the CHECK constraint and the partial index. No DB guard, trust the UI ‚Äî rejected, defence-in-depth matters here because the data drives tax filings.

## 2026-04-26 ‚Äî KDV summary is TRY-only; non-TRY VAT-bearing transactions are excluded with a visible count
**What:** `summarizeKdv` sums `vat_amount` only on rows where `currency === 'TRY'`. Any VAT-bearing row in a foreign currency contributes to a `skipped_count` per period instead of being converted. The `/tax` page surfaces a persistent footer (expandable) listing excluded rows when `skipped_count > 0`. The CSV export also includes a leading notes row with the non-TRY count.
**Why:** KDV is filed in TRY and the frozen-FX rule (2026-04-23) bars silent conversion of historical amounts. **Why:** A shipment in transit means every order on it has, by definition, been shipped ‚Äî keeping order status in sync with shipment status without manual per-order clicks reflects reality. Hard-blocking on inquiry/quoted (rather than silently skipping or auto-accepting) protects against the user accidentally putting unfinished negotiations on a real shipment: those orders shouldn't be on the manifest at all, and the loud failure is the right signal. The atomic-rollback pattern matches the proforma-import precedent ‚Äî Supabase's JS client doesn't expose multi-statement transactions, so we approximate atomicity in app code.
**Alternatives:** Silently skip non-cascadable orders ‚Äî rejected, hides a data-quality problem the user needs to see. Auto-promote inquiry/quoted to accepted as part of the cascade ‚Äî rejected, that decision belongs to the sales side, not the logistics side. Cascade at `arrived` instead of `in_transit` ‚Äî rejected, "shipped" semantically means "left the origin", not "reached the destination".

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî in_transit ‚Üí arrived is operational metadata only; no order or billing side effects
**What:** Advancing a shipment from `in_transit` to `arrived` only flips the shipment's status. Linked orders stay at `shipped` ‚Äî they are not auto-promoted to `delivered`. The billing transaction is not refreshed (and any further edit that would refresh it is blocked by the `arrived` lock). Per-order delivery confirmation, partial delivery, and any post-arrival adjustments are out of scope for this wave; they will be handled by future per-order UI plus adjustment transactions.
**Why:** Arrival at port ‚â† delivery to customer. A container can sit at customs for weeks, deliveries can be partial, and individual orders may be released to the customer on different dates. Auto-flipping every order to `delivered` on arrival would invent data the system doesn't actually know. Locking billing at this point matches the principle that the original invoice is now a historical document ‚Äî corrections live in adjustment transactions, not in edits to the original row.
**Alternatives:** Auto-cascade orders `shipped ‚Üí delivered` on arrival ‚Äî rejected, fabricates delivery confirmations the business doesn't have. Allow continued billing edits past `arrived` ‚Äî rejected, undermines the "invoice is final once goods land" principle and would require versioning the billing row. Treat `arrived` as a no-op status (skip it entirely) ‚Äî rejected, the operational milestone is genuinely useful for tracking and is the natural anchor for the billing lock.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Proforma import: user-driven external LLM + JSON paste, no app-side LLM calls

The manual copy-paste step happens only 4‚Äì5 times per month, so friction cost is low. The prompt template is rendered live from the Zod schema, so schema changes automatically propagate to the prompt the user sees.
**Alternatives:** Server-side extraction via the Anthropic API ‚Äî rejected for cost and key management. Client-side extraction ‚Äî rejected, exposes the key. Manual single-line entry ‚Äî rejected, doesn't scale to 40-line proformas.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Every proforma line creates a new product; no matching against existing catalog

**Why:** Proforma imports happen 4‚Äì5 times per month and are typically for genuinely new products. Adding a matching step would create friction for the common case to avoid a rare duplicate. The user explicitly opted for this simplicity; duplicates, if they appear, will be cleaned up manually later.
**Alternatives:** Provide the catalog to the LLM for matching ‚Äî rejected, heavier prompt and matching uncertainty. App-side fuzzy name matching ‚Äî rejected, same friction concern.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Proforma PDF not stored; ingestion is ephemeral
**What:** The proforma PDF itself is never uploaded to the app. Only the extracted JSON is pasted in, and even that is discarded after the atomic insert completes. No `supplier_proformas` table, no storage bucket, no attached file on any order.
**Why:** The user explicitly chose Option 1 ("pure batch-import UI, no stored entity") in Session 1. The value is in the resulting order lines, not in the source document. If source-document tracking ever becomes useful later, promotion to a stored entity is non-breaking.
**Alternatives:** Store PDF on the order as a reference ‚Äî rejected, mixes "the proforma" concept (supplier-side) with "the order" concept (customer-side). Dedicated proformas table with lines ‚Äî rejected, too heavy for a 4‚Äì5-times-per-month batch tool.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Proforma import mutation: insert-then-rollback-on-failure, not a single PG transaction
**What:** The batch mutation creates products and order lines via sequential Supabase inserts, tracking created ids. On any failure, it deletes everything it already created. This is not a true ACID transaction, but approximates atomicity at the application level.
**Why:** Supabase's JS client doesn't expose multi-statement transactions. The alternatives (a PL/pgSQL function the user runs once, or a server-side transaction via a custom endpoint) add operational complexity disproportionate to the risk. Inserts of newly-minted rows are the safe case for this pattern: there's nothing for the rollback to race against.
**Alternatives:** PL/pgSQL function wrapping BEGIN/COMMIT ‚Äî rejected for setup complexity; revisit if partial-failure cleanup ever misbehaves. Custom Next.js server action using direct PG connection ‚Äî rejected, leaks DB credentials server-side unnecessarily.

Keep V1 alongside V2 ‚Äî rejected, two tables answering the same question. Patch V1 incrementally ‚Äî rejected, would force the new constraints (snapshot columns, CHECK invariants, new FKs) to live as optional, defeating the point.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Inquiry is an order status, not a separate entity
**What:** Orders use a single 7-value `status` enum (`inquiry`, `quoted`, `accepted`, `in_production`, `shipped`, `delivered`, `cancelled`). A brand-new customer inquiry is created as an `orders` row with `status='inquiry'` and zero or more `order_details` lines; it progresses through quoted/accepted on the same row. No separate `inquiries` or `quotes` table.
**Why:** Every inquiry either becomes a real order or gets cancelled ‚Äî splitting into a separate table would force a "promote inquiry to order" copy step that loses line-item continuity and doubles the surface area for product snapshots, attachments, and status history. Treating it as a status keeps the lifecycle linear and keeps all documents/attachments on one row.
**Alternatives:** Separate `inquiries` table that morphs into an `orders` row ‚Äî rejected, introduces a schema-level transition and splits queries. Treat quoted-but-not-accepted as a draft flag on orders ‚Äî rejected, flags don't compose into a status pipeline; three-plus flags would be needed.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Order lines snapshot the product at line-creation time; later product edits don't propagate
**What:** `order_details` carries `product_name_snapshot`, `product_description_snapshot`, `product_photo_snapshot`, `unit_snapshot`, `cbm_per_unit_snapshot`, `weight_kg_per_unit_snapshot`, and the 5 packaging fields (`packaging_type`, `package_{length,width,height}_cm`, `units_per_package`). The FK `product_id` stays on the row for joins, but reports of "what was on that order" read the snapshot columns.
**Why:** A quoted/accepted order is a contract with the customer. If the operator renames a product, adjusts packaging, or swaps photos on the Products page, historical quotes must still reproduce exactly what was offered. Snapshotting makes the order row self-describing; it also lets operators intentionally override a field per-line (e.g. custom box dims for one shipment) without creating a product variant.
**Alternatives:** Read-through joins to `products` at display time ‚Äî rejected, a later product edit silently rewrites the history of every order. Versioned products table ‚Äî rejected, heavy schema for a small set of editable fields; snapshotting is the minimum that solves the problem.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Orders carry two shipment FKs: `shipment_id` (physical) and `billing_shipment_id` (financial)
**What:** `orders.shipment_id` points at the shipment the goods physically travel on; `orders.billing_shipment_id` points at the shipment whose invoice line the order is billed under. On initial assign, `billing_shipment_id` defaults to match `shipment_id`. When a shipment is booked but an order rolls over to a later shipment, the physical pointer moves but the billing pointer stays, so the customer is billed on the original shipment.
**Why:** "Ship it next month but bill it on this month's proforma" is a real operator workflow ‚Äî the customer has already committed to paying on the earlier shipment's invoice even though production slipped. Collapsing into one FK would force a choice: either the invoice rewrites when orders roll over (wrong), or the shipping manifest lies about what's on the boat (also wrong). Two FKs is the minimum that models both facts independently.
**Alternatives:** Single `shipment_id` with a boolean "billed-separately" flag ‚Äî rejected, can't point at a different shipment. Per-shipment `invoice_lines` with explicit order‚Üíline mapping ‚Äî rejected, heavier schema and doesn't solve the common case any better than two FKs.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Cancelling an order detaches from both shipments (NULLs `shipment_id` and `billing_shipment_id`)
**What:** The `cancelOrder` mutation sets `status='cancelled'`, stamps `cancelled_at=now()` and `cancellation_reason`, and clears both `shipment_id` and `billing_shipment_id` in the same update. A DB CHECK `(status='cancelled') = (cancelled_at IS NOT NULL)` guarantees status/timestamp stay consistent even if a future code path bypasses the mutation.
**Why:** A cancelled order shouldn't show up in shipment manifests or invoice totals ‚Äî leaving the FKs set would require every shipment-level aggregate to filter `status != 'cancelled'`, and the "orders in this shipment" list would need to hide cancelled rows everywhere. Nulling on cancel makes cancelled orders invisible to shipment queries by default; the `cancellation_reason` + `cancelled_at` columns preserve the audit trail.
**Alternatives:** Leave FKs set and filter on status everywhere ‚Äî rejected, every join has to remember the filter and one missed spot leaks cancelled orders into totals. Block cancellation once assigned ‚Äî rejected, real cancellations happen after assignment all the time.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Shipment `draft ‚Üí booked` gated on ‚â•1 linked order; later transitions ungated (Wave 1)

**Alternatives:** Allow empty bookings ‚Äî rejected, always a user error. Wire all cascades now ‚Äî rejected, shipment_billing transaction format and currency rules aren't locked yet; half-correct cascades would corrupt data that's hard to unwind.

## 2026-04-23 ‚Äî Two separate storage buckets: `order-attachments` and `shipment-documents`

Putting it on the product lets one edit fix every future proposal/proforma; putting it on each line would require re-entering it per order and invite drift. If a one-off order ever needs a different code, the snapshot column can be added later without re-entering existing data.

This reverses the plan's original "always shown, 60% opacity" approach.
**Why:** Early seeding left ~20 zero-balance placeholders (old test data, closed-out holdings) that pushed real rows below the fold even when dimmed. Hiding them by default makes the list reflect live positions; the toggle preserves access without a DB-side purge.
**Alternatives:** Keep dimmed-always ‚Äî rejected, clutter outweighed the find-old-SKU benefit that motivated the same pattern on products. Delete zero-balance rows ‚Äî rejected, movements history still references them; deletion belongs in an explicit cleanup pass, not as a side effect of list rendering.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Single "Refresh rates" button covers FX + non-fiat prices, `Promise.allSettled` so one failure doesn't block the other
**What:** One header button refreshes Frankfurter fiat rates, CoinPaprika crypto prices, and gold-api.com metal prices in parallel. The mutation uses `Promise.allSettled` ‚Äî if CoinPaprika is down, FX still updates, and vice versa. Separate toasts surface each side's outcome; a combined "Refreshed N FX + M price(s)" success toast fires when at least one leg succeeds.
**Why:** Users think "refresh my numbers," not "refresh fiat then crypto then metal." Two or three buttons would push the cognitive load onto the user for no gain. Splitting the fetches via `allSettled` keeps the UX simple without coupling failure modes ‚Äî a dead crypto API shouldn't leave fiat stale too.
**Alternatives:** One button, `Promise.all` ‚Äî rejected, one 5xx leaves everything stale. Three buttons (FX / Crypto / Metal) ‚Äî rejected, UI noise for zero real benefit; the user's mental model is "rates," not "which upstream."

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Non-fiat prices: CoinPaprika for crypto, gold-api.com for metals, hardcoded ticker maps, unknowns surfaced via toast
**What:** `refreshPrices` writes to `price_snapshots` using two upstreams. Crypto asset codes are resolved through a hardcoded `COINPAPRIKA_IDS` map (BTC‚Üíbtc-bitcoin, AVAX‚Üíavax-avalanche, ‚Ä¶) ‚Äî ~20 common tickers. Metals (XAU/XAG/Altƒ±n/G√ºm√º≈ü and their `(gr)` variants) are resolved through `METAL_ASSET_MAP` with a `{ metal, unit }` shape; gold-api.com returns USD/oz, which we divide by 31.1034768 for gram-denominated holdings. Unknown asset codes (e.g. KPA, KPC Kuveyt T√ºrk fund tickers) are collected into a `skipped` list and surfaced via a "No price source for: ‚Ä¶" toast instead of silently producing `‚Äî`.
**Why:** CoinGecko/Binance/Coinbase are all CORS-blocked for this project's browser context; CoinPaprika was the only free, no-key, CORS-open endpoint that returned real numbers. gold-api.com was the single metals source that worked without an API key and covered both gold and silver. Hardcoded symbol‚Üíid maps avoid an extra `/coins/list` round-trip (~6 MB) just to discover that BTC means bitcoin. The "skipped" toast is a deliberate nudge ‚Äî silent `‚Äî` looked like a bug when really the asset_code wasn't a recognized ticker.
**Alternatives:** CoinGecko ‚Äî rejected, CORS-blocked in practice. Binance `/api/v3/ticker/price` ‚Äî rejected, same. A generic fetch of `/coins` to build the id map dynamically ‚Äî deferred; the hardcoded list covers the user's current portfolio and is trivial to extend.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî "Altƒ±n" and "Altƒ±n(gr)" both interpreted as gold grams
**What:** `METAL_ASSET_MAP` maps asset_code `"Altƒ±n"` and `"Altƒ±n(gr)"` both to `{ metal: "gold", unit: "gram" }`. Silver codes `"G√ºm√º≈ü"` / `"G√ºm√º≈ü(gr)"` the same way.
**Why:** In Turkish treasury practice, "Altƒ±n" without a suffix almost always means gram altƒ±n ‚Äî the common unit for Kasa holdings. The `(gr)` variant is explicit; the bare form is overwhelmingly also grams. Treating them identically keeps the price-refresh path simple and matches how the user's existing holdings are sized (80, 70, 250 ‚Äî implausible as troy ounces, plausible as grams).
**Alternatives:** Treat `"Altƒ±n"` as troy ounce ‚Äî rejected, doesn't match Turkish convention. Treat `"Altƒ±n"` as ambiguous and skip ‚Äî rejected, user explicitly flagged the missing price as a problem. Ask on every refresh ‚Äî rejected, UX noise; the user can rename the asset_code to something standard (XAU, GOLD) if they want oz pricing.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Treasury movements as a new table, not on `transactions`
**What:** Quantity changes on holdings (opening, deposit, withdraw, transfer, trade, adjustment) live in a new `treasury_movements` table. The V1 `transactions` table is untouched and reserved for the accounting module.
**Why:** Treasury.md's model is "one row = one quantity change to one holding." V1 `transactions` is shaped for AR/AP/VAT/receipts ‚Äî a different entity. Mixing them would force every treasury query to filter noise and every accounting query to tolerate empty invoice fields.
**Alternatives:** Extend `transactions` with new columns ‚Äî rejected, accumulates dead weight. Rename and repurpose `transactions` ‚Äî rejected, existing V1 data has a different shape.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Signed quantity, one row per leg, `group_id` links transfers and trades
**What:** `treasury_movements.quantity` is signed. A transfer or trade writes two rows with the same `group_id` and opposite signs. Balance of an account = `SUM(quantity)`.
**Why:** Matches treasury.md literally ("transfers create two linked rows") and keeps the rule that every row represents exactly one change to one holding. `group_id` is lighter than a dedicated linking table; promote to its own table later if we ever need to attach facts (rate, counterparty) to the pair itself.
**Alternatives:** Direction column + unsigned quantity ‚Äî rejected, every balance query needs a CASE. Separate `transfers` / `trades` tables ‚Äî rejected, premature normalization.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Ortak movement type stub captured at entry, classified later
**What:** `treasury_movements.ortak_movement_type` is a nullable enum (`partner_loan_in` / `partner_loan_out` / `profit_share`). The UI requires it only when the account's custody has `custody_locations.requires_movement_type = true` (currently just Ortak).
**Why:** Decisions.md already committed to this classification being needed for the accounting module's GL posting. Backfilling it later would mean reclassifying every historical Ortak movement from memory ‚Äî capture at entry instead. The flag on `custody_locations` keeps the rule data-driven, not hardcoded to "Ortak."
**Alternatives:** Defer entirely ‚Äî rejected, backfill pain. Hardcode the requirement to the string "Ortak" ‚Äî rejected, brittle.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî FX refresh writes a new snapshot row per currency, never updates in place
**What:** Clicking the FX refresh button upserts one row per fiat currency into `fx_snapshots` on the `(currency_code, snapshot_date, source)` unique key ‚Äî one row per currency per day per source, with `fetched_at` advanced on same-day refreshes. Prior days' rows are retained, giving a day-grain audit trail. The USD display uses the latest snapshot per currency. API used: `api.frankfurter.dev/v1` (the `frankfurter.app` hostname still works but 301-redirects cross-origin, which browsers can't follow for `fetch`).
**Why:** Audit trail for what rate was used when. Keeps the "USD view ‚Äî rates from {date}" stamp honest and makes "why did this value jump overnight?" trivially debuggable.
**Alternatives:** Update-in-place the latest row ‚Äî rejected, loses history.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî V1 columns on `accounts` (`account_type`, `subtype`, `shares`) left untouched pending review
**What:** Three columns on `accounts` predate the unified-holdings redesign and don't appear in treasury.md. Leave them in place; don't write to them from new code; revisit when their current use (if any) is understood.
**Why:** Dropping them blind risks breaking a V1 query path or losing data. Keeping them noisily risks confusion for anyone reading the schema cold. A short audit later beats either failure mode now.
**Alternatives:** Drop immediately ‚Äî rejected, unknown downstream use. Migrate data into the new model now ‚Äî rejected, we don't yet know what the data means.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Product form as a step wizard, not a single long dialog
**What:** `ProductFormDialog` renders one section at a time (Basics ‚Üí Client-facing ‚Üí Photo ‚Üí Pricing ‚Üí Logistics ‚Üí Packaging) with Back/Next controls and clickable progress pills. Next triggers Zod validation scoped to the current step's fields via `form.trigger(fields)`; the Save button only appears on the last step. Pressing Enter on an intermediate step calls `goNext()` instead of submitting.
**Why:** The flat form had 15+ fields and forced vertical scrolling in the dialog. Breaking it into steps keeps each screen small and lets the user skip optional sections (Photo, Pricing, Logistics, Packaging) without seeing them.
**Alternatives:** Tabs ‚Äî rejected, tabs look like "pick one" rather than "fill in order" and hide validation errors on other tabs. A single scrolling form with a sticky section nav ‚Äî rejected, still long; nav added visual weight without fixing the core problem.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Client-generated product UUID before insert
**What:** The form dialog generates a `crypto.randomUUID()` and holds it in a ref for the lifetime of the "new product" dialog. Image uploads use that ID as the storage folder; the insert then passes the same ID to `createProduct`.
**Why:** Image upload needs a stable folder (`product-photos/{product_id}/‚Ä¶`) before the DB row exists. Without a pre-generated ID we'd have to insert a placeholder row, upload, then update ‚Äî two round-trips plus an orphan row if the user cancels.
**Alternatives:** Upload to a temp folder and move on save ‚Äî rejected, Supabase Storage has no rename/move API, only copy+delete. Insert-then-upload-then-update ‚Äî rejected, extra round-trips and orphan rows if the upload step fails.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Product image column stores the storage path, not the public URL
**What:** `products.product_image` stores a relative path like `uuid/filename.jpg`. The full URL is computed at render time via `supabase.storage.from('product-photos').getPublicUrl(path)` in `productImageUrl()`.
**Why:** Bucket name stays in config (`PRODUCT_IMAGE_BUCKET`). If the bucket is renamed or moved behind a CDN later, no row-level rewrite is needed.
**Alternatives:** Store full URL ‚Äî rejected, couples every row to the current public-URL shape.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Image upload deferred until form submit, not on file-pick
**What:** Picking a file sets local state (`pendingFile`) and a `URL.createObjectURL()` preview. The actual upload to Supabase Storage happens inside the save mutation, after Zod validation passes. Replacing an image deletes the old file; removing an image deletes the file and sets `product_image = null`.
**Why:** Upload-on-pick would orphan files in storage every time a user cancels the dialog or picks a file and abandons the save. Defer-and-batch guarantees storage state matches DB state.
**Alternatives:** Upload immediately on pick with a "mark for deletion on cancel" cleanup pass ‚Äî rejected, cleanup is racy (page close, network loss) and leaves dangling files.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Built `Combobox` primitive on Popover + Command instead of extending `Select`
**What:** New component at `src/components/ui/combobox.tsx` ‚Äî a searchable dropdown with optional inline create, built from shadcn's Popover + Command (cmdk). Used for category and supplier pickers.
**Why:** shadcn's `Select` is not searchable. Products has two fields (category, supplier) where the lists will grow beyond what's scannable without type-ahead, and category also needs inline "Create ‚Ä¶" ‚Äî neither fits Select.
**Alternatives:** Use plain `Select` and accept slow scanning ‚Äî rejected, worse UX as the lists grow. Use a third-party combobox (react-select, downshift) ‚Äî rejected, the CLAUDE.md rule is "copy shadcn components, don't wrap libraries."

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî KDV rate is a fixed dropdown (0 / 1 / 10 / 20), not free input
**What:** `kdv_rate` form field is a `<Select>` with exactly four options: 0%, 1%, 10%, 20%. Zod refines the value against `KDV_RATES` in `src/lib/supabase/types.ts`.
**Why:** These are the four official Turkish VAT rates. Free text would let typos (`18`, `19`, `21`) into the catalog, which then flow into every invoice generated from the product.
**Alternatives:** Number input with validation ‚Äî rejected, same typos still possible before validation fires. DB-level `CHECK` constraint ‚Äî can be added later, but the UI guard is the real line of defence.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Inline category creation inside the combobox, no category management UI
**What:** Typing in the Category combobox shows a "Create '{query}'" row when no match exists. Selecting it fires `createProductCategory` and auto-selects the new category. There is no separate page or dialog for managing categories.
**Why:** Categories are lightweight labels (name only). A dedicated management screen is dead weight for a flat list that's created as a side-effect of adding products. Inline create keeps the flow in one place.
**Alternatives:** Separate `/products/categories` page with CRUD ‚Äî rejected for MVP, not worth the UI. Editable categories (rename/delete) ‚Äî deferred; if a rename is ever needed, Supabase dashboard handles it.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Products list defaults to Active only, inactive rows shown dimmed not hidden
**What:** The active filter on `/products` defaults to "Active only". Switching to "Include inactive" adds inactive rows to the same list, rendered at 60% opacity. There is no inactive-only view.
**Why:** The common case is "show me things I actually sell." Inactive products are rare exceptions ‚Äî surfacing them in the same list (dimmed) is faster than context-switching to a separate view.
**Alternatives:** Three-way toggle (active / inactive / all) ‚Äî rejected, adds a state that's almost never used. Hide inactive entirely ‚Äî rejected, then users can't find old SKUs to reactivate without a schema dive.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Dev auth bypass environment switch
**What:** `NEXT_PUBLIC_DISABLE_AUTH=true` in `.env.local` short-circuits the auth middleware and stamps `created_by`/`edited_by` as null. Lives in `src/lib/auth-mode.ts`. Must remain unset in production.
**Why:** Speeds up local development ‚Äî no need to sign in every hot reload. The null audit stamping makes dev-mode writes visually obvious in the DB.
**Alternatives:** Seed a test user and always log in ‚Äî rejected, more friction day-to-day.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Soft delete for contacts via `deleted_at`
**What:** Deleting a contact sets `deleted_at = now()` rather than removing the row. Every list query filters `.is("deleted_at", null)`.
**Why:** Deleted contacts may still be referenced by historical transactions/orders. Hard delete would orphan FK references.
**Alternatives:** Hard delete with FK cascade ‚Äî rejected, destroys audit trail.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Balance currency constrained in code to TRY/EUR/USD/GBP
**What:** The TypeScript type in `src/lib/supabase/types.ts` narrows balance_currency to these four values, even though the DB column likely accepts any text.
**Why:** Matches actual business use. Prevents typos like "USD " or "Usd" creating silent bugs.
**Alternatives:** Lock at the DB level with a CHECK constraint ‚Äî better, but can be added later without schema pain.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî "No country" bucket in country-grouped view
**What:** Contacts with null country_code group under a `__none` bucket labelled "No country" with a üè≥ emoji.
**Why:** Grouping must not silently drop rows. Explicit bucket surfaces missing data so Yusuf notices and fixes it.

## 2026-04-22 ‚Äî Both floating + header "Add contact" buttons on desktop
**What:** Desktop shows both a header "Add contact" button and a floating + button bottom-right. Mobile shows only the floating button.
**Why:** Header button is discoverable for new users; floating button is fast for power use.
**Alternatives:** Pick one ‚Äî deferred. Revisit if it feels cluttered in practice.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Unified accounts: one table for every asset type
**What:** The `accounts` table holds fiat bank balances, crypto, physical metals, and fund positions ‚Äî rows tagged by `asset_type` (fiat | crypto | metal | fund). Old `currency` column renamed `asset_code` since it now holds BTC, Altƒ±n, KTJ, etc., not just currencies.
**Why:** Treasury.md's "quantity is the source of truth" principle applies equally to grams of gold and units of KTJ as it does to dollars. Splitting into per-asset-class tables would duplicate schema for the same pattern.
**Alternatives:** Separate `bank_accounts`, `crypto_holdings`, `fund_positions` tables ‚Äî rejected, same shape repeated three times.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Table name stays `accounts`, not renamed to `holdings`
**What:** Keep `accounts` as the table name even though treasury.md's sketch calls it "Holdings."
**Why:** Many V1 references (transactions.from_account_id, to_account_id) already point at `accounts`. Renaming would cascade through the codebase for cosmetic gain.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Custody location as editable lookup (≈ûirket / Ortak / Kasa + off-site)
**What:** New `custody_locations` table (id, name, location_type: bank | physical). Accounts link to it via `custody_location_id`. List is editable in the app ‚Äî Yusuf can add GAP 1/4, Kasa √áekme, etc. without a migration.
**Why:** Treasury.md requires custody to be a first-class dimension independent of asset type. Editability avoids schema changes every time a new safe or off-site location is added.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Price snapshots unified across asset types
**What:** Single `price_snapshots` table (asset_code, snapshot_date, price, price_currency, source). V1's `fund_prices` migrated in and dropped.
**Why:** Gold, crypto, and fund prices all have the same shape ‚Äî a daily price in some currency with a source. One table keeps the display logic uniform.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî FX snapshots separate from price snapshots
**What:** `fx_snapshots(currency_code, snapshot_date, rate_to_usd, source, fetched_at)`. Written only when the user taps the FX refresh button (per treasury.md ‚Äî no background refresh).
**Why:** FX has two distinct jobs (wallet display vs. frozen client-balance rate) and a different refresh cadence than asset prices. Combining them would conflate "price of an asset" with "value of a currency against USD."

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Contact types: customer, supplier, logistics, other
**What:** Contact `type` constrained to these four values. Government dropped. Partners live in their own table, not in contacts.
**Why:** Mainstream ERPs don't treat governments as contacts ‚Äî tax offices are registries referenced in filings, not transactable parties. Partners have their own data shape (ownership %, profit-share) and their own UI section.
**Alternatives:** Keep Government (rejected ‚Äî no transactions attach to it). Fold Partners into contacts (rejected ‚Äî different fields, already separate in the app).

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Single type per contact
**What:** One contact has exactly one type. A company that is both supplier and customer is duplicated.
**Why:** Has never happened in Yusuf's practice. Multi-role modelling would cost UI and schema complexity for a non-problem.
**Alternatives:** Multi-role via array or join table ‚Äî rejected.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Country as controlled ISO list
**What:** `contacts.country_code` references a `countries` lookup table keyed by ISO-3166-1 alpha-2 codes (TR, ML, CM‚Ä¶). Lookup holds display name and flag.
**Why:** Free text breaks country-grouped views and flag rendering (T√ºrkiye / Turkey / TR become three groups).
**Alternatives:** Free text (rejected).

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Tax ID stored on all contacts; Tax Office Turkish-only in UI
**What:** `tax_id` is a field on every contact, required for customers (needed in Y√ºkleme Talimatƒ±), optional otherwise. `tax_office` is stored on every contact but only shown in forms when country = T√ºrkiye.
**Why:** Y√ºkleme Talimatƒ± requires client tax ID regardless of country. Vergi Dairesi (tax office) is a Turkish tax concept only ‚Äî showing it for foreign contacts clutters the form.
**Alternatives:** Tax ID only for Turkish contacts (rejected ‚Äî foreign customers need it too for shipping docs).

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Contact notes split: static field + timestamped activity log
**What:** Each contact has a `notes` text field for persistent facts, plus a separate `contact_notes` table for timestamped interaction entries. Activity entries are append-only.
**Why:** Static context ("pays late") and dated incidents ("21 Apr disagreement") are different needs. Mixing them loses the date anchor on incidents.
**Alternatives:** Single notes field with hand-typed dates (rejected ‚Äî unstructured, not queryable).

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Packaging defaults on the product, overridable per order
**What:** Each product has a usual packaging config. When added to an order line, that config copies over and can be edited for that specific order without touching the product master.
**Why:** Most products always ship the same way (tiles on 120√ó80 pallets, etc.), so a default covers nearly all cases. Occasional one-offs (break a pallet into loose boxes) need to be captured without polluting the product master.
**Alternatives:** (a) packaging only on product ‚Äî too rigid; (b) only on order line ‚Äî repetitive entry; (c) only on shipment ‚Äî too late, proposal needs it.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Product versioning: snapshot at order time (Option A)
**What:** `products` table holds only the current state. When a product is added to an order line, relevant fields (name, price, description, photo, packaging) are copied onto the line. Editing a product later doesn't retroactively change old orders.
**Why:** Old orders must stay faithful to what was sold. Snapshotting keeps schema simple (one row per product) while preserving historical truth.
**Alternatives:** Option B ‚Äî version rows per material edit, order lines reference a specific version. Heavier schema, allows a product-history view. Rejected because per-order fidelity matters, product-side history doesn't.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Inquiry is the first status of an order, not a separate entity
**What:** An order can exist from the inquiry stage with minimal fields (client + product). It progresses: inquiry ‚Üí quoted ‚Üí accepted ‚Üí in production ‚Üí shipped ‚Üí done.
**Why:** Avoids two near-identical entities and a "promote inquiry to order" step. Same row, earlier state.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Client balance tracked per client, not per order
**What:** One running balance per client. Payments and order charges flow against it. No per-order balance.
**Why:** Clients pre-load funds to build up for future orders ($30k sent when only $10k of orders exist). Per-order balance would be over-engineered for this pattern.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Client balance currency fixed per client
**What:** Each client has a default currency. Their balance is always kept in that currency. Payments in other currencies convert at receipt-time rate.
**Why:** One consistent view of what the client has paid and what's owed. Avoids multi-currency-per-client mess.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Payment FX rate captured at transaction time
**What:** When a payment arrives, the FX rate used to convert it into the client's balance currency is stored on the transaction row and never recomputed.
**Why:** A client-balance credit is a promise made on a specific date. It must not silently move when rates change. Distinct from treasury wallet display, where FX is applied at display time from snapshots.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Freight is a separate line, not allocated into per-product profit
**What:** Orders are sold EXW. Freight is quoted and invoiced as its own line, not amortised back into product margins.
**Why:** Matches how Yusuf already sells. Keeps per-product profit clean.

## 2026-04-21 ‚Äî Orders sit inside shipments; shipping the shipment ships all orders inside
**What:** A shipment groups one or more orders. Order status is per order, but when the shipment ships, every order inside transitions together.
**Why:** One container often carries multiple consolidated orders. Status must cascade from the shipment event.
